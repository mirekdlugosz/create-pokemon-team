Setting up the team

At the very top of the page, select Pokémon game you are playing and whether you have one or more Pokémon transferred from earlier generation. Second option is important because list of moves that Pokémon can learn is different in each game and sometimes Pokémon lose the ability to learn certain moves. However, if your Pokémon learned that move and then was transferred to newer game, it can still use it.

You can select moves that are not legitimately available in certain games. Up until generation VII, it was not possible to transfer Pokémon out of generations I and II. Even today, they can be transferred only to generation VII games. But nothing on this website prevents you from saying you have Tauros with Stomp in Pokémon Black (a move it could learn only in generation I).

Use the form below to select Pokémon in your team and their moves. You can type to narrow down the list of items that are displayed. If list of moves is empty, then refresh the page.

Team overview

Team overview displays all Pokémon types and little table for each of them.

  • № resisting - number of Pokémon in your team that resist attack of given type (effectiveness of an attack is below 1). Your Pokémon resists the attack if message "It's not very effective" or "It had no effect" is displayed when it is hit. Number will be red if there are no Pokémon with resistance in your team. Well-balanced team will have at least one Pokémon resisting each type of attack.
  • № weak to - number of Pokémon in your team that are weak to attack of given type (effectiveness of an attack is above 1). Your Pokémon is weak to the attack if message "It's super effective" is displayed when it is hit.
  • № of SE moves - number of super effective moves that all of your Pokémon have against given type. Well-balanced team will have at least one super effective move against each type.
  • Has counter? - counter is Pokémon that resists attack of given type and can hit Pokémon of that type with super effective move. It should be your main choice when facing opponent of that type. If you do not have counter for given type, "No" will be displayed in red. Well-balanced team will have counters against majority of types.

Team type effectiveness

In Team type effectiveness section, you can see how each of your Pokémon fare against each type.

You can click Pokémon name to hide information about it. This makes it easier to quickly jump between main form and analysis results.

  • Effect - effectiveness of attack of given type on that Pokémon. Numbers below 1 mean that your Pokémon resists that type and are displayed in green. Numbers above 1 mean that your Pokémon is weak against that type and are displayed in red. When facing opponent of that type, usually you will want to send Pokémon with resistance - or, at least, without weakness.
  • SE - number of super effective moves that your Pokémon has against that type. Numbers above zero are displayed in green. Usually it's good idea to send Pokémon with at least one super effective move when facing opponent of given type.
  • NE - number of moves with neutral effectiveness, that is moves that are neither strong nor weak. They are not as strong as super effective moves, but will do when your Pokémon resists given type and you can't use other Pokémon.
  • NVE - number of not very effective moves that your Pokémon has against that type. Most of the time you want to avoid using them against opponent of given type.
